Why this matters

Real shop feedback changed the product fast.

This case study is meant to stay alive. As Goldie's uses the app, DrinkFlow can document what owners actually needed: clearer ticket order, cleaner drink counts, better reports, downloadable files, and advice that changes with the numbers instead of repeating canned text.

Older tickets first

The owner noticed newer tickets could distract staff. KDS columns were updated so older tickets stay at the top and newer tickets land at the bottom.

Drink counts cleaned up

Retail items like bagged coffee, soap, shower steamers, and packaged goods were kept out of drink analytics so the owner view stays useful.

Reports became owner-ready

Owner reports now support CSV, Excel, and branded PDF downloads with charts, hourly order patterns, and drink-shop guidance.

Honest by design

A small tool from a small studio.

DrinkFlow is not a large restaurant-tech platform with layers of sales, onboarding, and support tickets. It is a practical custom KDS built by Studio Samantha for small drink shops that want clear screens, useful owner reports, and direct help after launch.

What that means for customers

Setup can be tailored around the shop's actual people, menu, counters, devices, and Square® setup instead of forcing every business into the same template.

It also means the public case study can show the real improvement loop: a shop notices something, the app changes, and the version history explains why.
