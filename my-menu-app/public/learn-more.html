Buyer questions

Clear answers before setup.

Coffee shops, smoothie bars, tea shops, bakeries with drink volume, and counters already using Square® for orders or payments.

The goal is not to replace Square®. It is to give drink shops a cleaner KDS, cleaner drink analytics, and owner reports that do not need a spreadsheet rescue.

What does DrinkFlow have that Square® KDS does not focus on?

DrinkFlow focuses on drink categories, average drinks per order, 2+ drink order rate, daily owner notes, branded PDF reports, customer-facing drink displays, online pickup, kiosk paths, and custom setup.

Does it replace Square®?

No. DrinkFlow connects through the Square® API, so the shop should already use Square® for orders or payments.

Does DrinkFlow work with POS systems besides Square®?

Square® is the first live connector. Shopify, Clover, Lightspeed, and other POS workflows can be explored when a shop's order data is accessible through an API or webhook.

What screens work best?

Often, the cleanest setup is a TV or monitor for the shared board, with staff moving tickets from an iPad, phone, mouse, remote, or nearby control device.

Can customers see order numbers too?

Yes. A customer-facing orders-up display can be added for coffee shops, food trucks, and pickup counters that want customers to see when orders are ready.

Can DrinkFlow handle online ordering or a kiosk?

Online pickup and self-order kiosk flows are in beta. They use the same drink-focused menu rules as the KDS, including hot/iced choices, sizes, pickup timing, and Square® checkout.

What happens to retail items?

Retail items like merch, packaged goods, gifts, and add-ons can stay out of drink totals, so the owner report stays focused on drink behavior.
