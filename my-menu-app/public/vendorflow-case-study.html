Public case study

VendorFlow keeps preorder sales readable.

This public story follows a real summer souvenir shop that uses preorders to stay small-batch, reduce overproduction, and keep event pickup easy to follow. The case study stays public so shoppers and partners can see how the flow works.

What shoppers see

A clean preorder page, not a crowded booth.

The public page stays focused on the products, the pickup window, and the few details shoppers actually need. That makes it easier to preorder tees, totes, and hats without feeling like they are navigating a back-office tool.

Clear choices

Shoppers can see what is available, what is made in-house, and what will be ready for pickup.

Less waste

Smaller preorder runs reduce overproduction and keep the event stock closer to real demand.

What the setup proves

VendorFlow works when the booth needs to stay simple.

Fast pickup

Order numbers and simple status handling keep the line moving.

Inventory separation

Handmade items and regular retail items can be tracked differently.

Event-ready

The flow is built for fairs, artist alleys, and seasonal vendor setups.

Why it matters

VendorFlow should help shoppers preorder with confidence, help the booth stay organized, and help the maker bring only what makes sense to make.
